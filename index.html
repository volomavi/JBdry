JB's Tip of the Week

Tips for fueling during a run

Running is a simple sport. We put on some shorts and a tee, lace up our shoes and head out the door. But do we stop to think about hydration and nutrition in the same way that our km splits occupy our brain. Here's some tips on how to hydrate and eat fuel on a run.

Hydration

Keeping well hydrated is essential for maximum performance, to prevent cramping, improve recovery and help keep you focused. Take small sips often. This helps maintain your hydration and doesn't stress your stomach when you become thirsty and guzzle down half a bottle. Hydrate with electrolytes (as well as water). This will help replace electrolytes you are losing as you sweat (mainly salt). This will prevent cramping, and other more scientific problems. Hydrate all year. Adjust your hydration levels based on the temperature. Obviously when it's hot you need to drink...a lot! But you still need to drink in cold conditions, because you're still sweating.

Trail Talk

Lynn Valley: Norvan falls

An absolute classic route on the north shore for hikers and trail runners. This is a great trail for everyone! Runners looking to dip their toes into trail running will find a trail that has enough elevation to push yourself but be manageable, and experienced trail runners can utilise this to practise fast paced trail running or start their journey to more difficult trails.
